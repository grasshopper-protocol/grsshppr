# Personas & Non-Goals

Who Grasshopper is for, who it isn't, and what it deliberately will not become.

## Who it's for

### Mentee — "the intentional learner"
A tech or design professional (often early-to-mid career) who wants focused,
goal-driven guidance — not a one-off call. Values continuity, owns their goals,
willing to prepare. Frustrated by overwhelming directories and paywalls.

### Mentor — "the generous expert"
An experienced tech/design practitioner who wants to give back on their own
terms. Volunteers time, defines their own availability, cares about real impact
over vanity metrics. Will not tolerate a noisy, demanding, or salesy platform.

### Self-hoster / contributor — "the open-source believer"
Runs or extends Grasshopper. Wants a lean, legible codebase, no vendor lock-in,
and a transparent way to propose changes across code, design, and product.

## Core use cases

1. Mentee discovers a relevant mentor and books a session against a goal.
2. A mentoring pair builds continuity via shared notes across sessions.
3. A mentee tracks progress toward goals, attributed to the mentors who helped.
4. A mentor manages availability and incoming requests without friction.

## Non-goals (what Grasshopper will NOT become)

- **A marketplace.** No paid tiers, no take rate, no "premium mentors."
- **A directory of everything.** Tech & design focus, quality over quantity.
- **A social network.** No feeds, no follower counts, no engagement loops.
- **A surveillance product.** No analytics, tracking, or telemetry.
- **A maximalist platform.** Every feature earns its place; bloat is rejected.

These non-goals are load-bearing. Proposals that violate them need an RFC that
explicitly argues to change this document first.
